Stanley Martin Lieber

A.k.a. Stan Lee: The prolific comic-book writer.

Stan Lee Smiling and Waving

Early Life

Stan Lee was born as Stanley Martin Lieber in Manhattan, New York just three days after Christmas on December 28th, 1922. Stan grew up in a Romanian-Jewish family that strugged during the Great Depression. He found his escape in reading books, and watching movies. Particularly those revolving around a hero.

Early Career

Stan Lee dreamed of being a writer. So his uncle helped him get a job at Timely Comics, as an assistant. Most of his work involved making sure the artists were fed and pampered so they could work.

He made his writing debut with a filler story about Captain America, who we all now know and love. Shortly after this, due to internal politics, Lee who was only 19 at the time, was made editor for Timely Comics. Stan enlisted in the US Army and served from 1942 to 1945.

The Golden Age

After returning from service, Lee continued at his position as editor, but began writing Romance, Westerns, comedy, and Sci-fi. He was feeling unfulfilled at his job, and was considering quitting...

At this time in a parallel universe (the D.C. Comics Universe) superhero comics were beginning to take off. Martin Goodman, who was running Timely, wanted Stan Lee to come up with his own stable of superheroes to compete with D.C. Comics.

Lee proved his writing chops, by focusing on fundamentally flawed and human characters, instead of the idealistically perfect heroes who had come before. First Stan Lee and Jack Kirby created the Fantastic Four, then quickly after the Hulk, Thor, Iron Man, the X-Men, Daredevil, Doctor Strange, and everyone's favorite webslinger, Spider-Man
